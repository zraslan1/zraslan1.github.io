About

Thank you for taking the time to visit my portfolio!

I am a Geospatial Analyst specialized in interdisciplinary research and Historical GIS. I created this portfolio to highlight my research and career interests, educational and professional background, and examples of GIS projects I am or have been a part of. Ranging from LLD telecommunication network drafting to geospatially modeling community social ecology, my career as a GIS professional has focused on a variety of fields directly impacting community well-being. Currently, I work with the Massachusetts Development Finance Agency as a GIS Specialist. Notably, I’ve worked as a GIS Analyst for an environmental protection organization, a political campaign, civil engineering and energy corporations, and the Critical Health and Social Ecology Lab at Wesleyan University. My time as a GIS Specialist with the Critical Health and Social Ecology Lab in Wesleyan University’s Psychology department stands out to me as one of my most interesting and fulfilling roles. Specifically, this lab was analyzing a long-running survey of Chicago residents’ feelings on social well-being, community connectedness, happiness, and other indicators of socio-ecological health. Geographically analyzing the distribution of responses in the survey revealed spatial patterns that were otherwise impossible to see through statistical analysis and tables. This work inspires me to think about what the heart of a place means; how can the study of urban-economic geography, history, and GIS help us understand and improve community well-being?

In addition, I have been offering GIS and Digital Humanities tutoring services since 2014, founded a small Geospatial Consulting business (ClioGraphica) in 2015, and have been volunteering as a Mapper for the Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team (HOT) since Spring 2023.
